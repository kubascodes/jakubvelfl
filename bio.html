INTRO

I am a multi-talented person with a cross-disciplinary background. Instead of specialising in one field, I like variety, thinking beyond the box to see the big picture.

I studied languages, mathematics, business and later informatics. On top of that, I self-learned programming with a dream of becoming a web developer. Similarly, I put my nose into mechanics and electrics to learn how to fix bikes and to get my old bus back on the road. As I am deeply concerned by the environmental crisis, I continue to broaden my knowledge of topics around nature - sustainability - alternative socioeconomic systems. Last but not least, I nurture my creative side with strong interests in art — design — architecture. I believe learning is a continuous journey.

BEING A HUMAN BEING

We use a lot of labels in an attempt to capture our essence. We introduce ourselves by our name, profession, age, nationality... In trying to say who we are, we try to fit into a narrow definition of what it means to be [human]. I invite you to think beyond labels and definitions... Okay, but what exactly do I mean by that?

To me, there are lots of "I am" that capture a part of me and then there are things that "I like to" do.

At times, I am the geek behind the computer screen, coding and wrangling data to useful information, bringing innovative ideas to life. Likewise, I enjoy talking to people, thinking, reading, connecting interesting information to insights that lead to the discovery of new knowledge.

I also find pleasure in fixing broken things, it's quite fulfilling to figure out what's wrong, putting my skills and knowledge on the table to find a good solution that hopefully ... works! Being able to repair things is also a lifesafer when something goes wrong down the road, not mentioning it's better than buying new!

But hey, there is more to my story - life is not a constant, it's a story that is constantly unfolding through everyday living.

I'm a mountain biker, traveller, backpacker, hitchhiker driven by curiosity and discovery. I wonder what is behind the horizon. And then I go. Within these activities, I rejoice over being outdoors, deepening my connection with the natural world and my inner self through reflections. I call it disconnect to reconnect.

I like ... quite a lot of things! I like working with my hands, I like to exercise my brain and stretch my body beyond the zone of comfort to keep on trying new things. Taking small steps that lead to big things, growing and growing... I started climbing to face my fear of heights - hanging up on the rope, trusting myself and my climbing partner with life. Life is often about balance - slacklining helps me to find balance between the body and the mind - you fall when you're not in equilibrium. And then there's surfing - the secret to which is much deeper than what's obvious on the surface. Surfing teaches me to wait patiently for the right wave through careful observation of the ocean - yeah you know when it's coming ... paddle, push, stand up ... and you're riding on natural high, oh yeah!

Then there is the world of sounds, writing, little projects here and there, analog photography... I love playing the piano, recording natural soundscapes - feeling like a sonic explorer through outer worlds. I like to express myself in a creative way, inspired by the world around me and hopefully inspire others myself.

And of course, when the right season comes around, I harvest fruits and brew refreshments that quench thirst, replenish energy and put a smile on people's faces.

While distinct and seemingly unrelated, each of these "I am" and "I like to" experiences contributes tremendously towards my personal development, know-how and a balanced skillset overall.
